What the badges mean

HIRING DISPATCH Lead with the job posting in your opener. They're actively hiring for the exact role your software is designed to support.
NO FSM Qualification confirmed. They're booking through phone or web form, so there's no competitor to displace — just a greenfield install.
PAIN ## Reference the complaint directly. Reviews mention dispatch failures, missed callbacks, or abandoned jobs — position your product as the fix.
GROWTH ## They're scaling and the wheels are starting to wobble. Hiring posts, accelerating review volume, or momentum language in reviews.
DEMAND PULL ## They're winning on reputation, not process. Customers switching from competitors, owner named by first name in reviews — which means the owner is still running dispatch off a whiteboard.
THIN SAMPLE (#) Buying signals are present but based on a smaller data set. Still worth a call — just verify the situation hasn't changed.

How the score works

Each contractor is scored across seven dimensions drawn from independent public data sources: state licensing records, website technology fingerprinting, Google Jobs hiring posts, Google Business Profile review patterns, and AZ ROC bond filings. A multi-signal bonus rewards contractors where independent sources point the same direction — a single complaint could be a bad day, but a complaint plus a dispatcher hiring post plus customers switching from a competitor is a pattern. Contractors whose websites show modern booking platforms or whose customers praise automated workflows get points deducted — they've likely already bought. Scores are discounted when data samples are thin, so no single source can dominate the ranking. Contractors scoring 40+ are tagged TOP PRIORITY, 25–39 are STRONG INTENT, and below 25 are EMERGING.
